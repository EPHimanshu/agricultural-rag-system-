Support Price Scheme Type of scheme : Central Sector Scheme The Minimum Support Prices were announced by the Government of India for the first time in 1966 -67 for Wheat in the wake of the Green Revolution and extended harvest, to save the farmers from depleting profits. Since then, the MSP regime has been expanded to many crops. Minimum Support Price is the price at which government purchases crops from the farmers, whatever may be the price for the crops. The MSP is announced by the Government of India for 26 crops currently at the beginning of each season viz. Rabi and Kharif. They are as follows. • Cereals (7) - paddy, wheat, barley, jowar, bajra, maize and ragi • Pulses (5) - gram, arha
